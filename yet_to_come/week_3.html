"Yet To Come"

Week 3: 10.15.2022




The highlight of this week was the BTS concert in Busan, Korea. It was free for both those watching it in person and those live-streaming it (aka me.) I was a little disappointed when I read that the concert began at 6 PM KST (5 AM my time) because I love to sleep. However, I quickly decided to get up early to watch the concert anyway. Shockingly, I found myself filled with excitement when my alarm went off at 4:30 the next day. Only three songs into the show, the streaming server crashed due to the 50 million people watching and commenting on the live. I was crushed, especially considering I had gotten up so early to watch the show. However, soon I found a link to another streaming site that worked, and I was able to enjoy the rest of the concert.

The concert filled me with a unique sort of love and happiness that I haven’t felt in a long time. BTS is quite a special band, they have built a dedicated community of fans at a scarily large scale. The music in this concert spoke to me, especially the last song, “Yet to Come.” I have gone through many dark points where I didn’t want to be alive and saw no future for myself, and the fact that I’ve survived this far gives me mixed feelings. BTS also has faced many difficulties, including grueling practice hours and intense pressure to succeed. Especially earlier this year, when the media posted rumors about them breaking up, I was very upset. But when they released their new album “Proof” and sang the line “the best moment is yet to come,” I felt quite relieved and inspired. If they could keep going throughout multiple challenges and remain hopeful about the future, so could I.

I felt inspired by the concert, so I started writing a song called “Lavender Snow” about my love for BTS. I have attached the lyrics below. I also saw pictures of the drone show done after the concert, where hundreds of drones were programmed to depict the faces of BTS members and other related symbols in the night sky. I have tried to re-create some of those pictures with Aski art.
